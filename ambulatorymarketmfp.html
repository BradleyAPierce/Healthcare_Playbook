Document Workflow and Connectivity Challenges

  • Scanning documents to a patient's health record can be a complicated and disjointed process, often requiring multiple steps and unnecessary time indexing to the EHR, resulting in additional operational costs.
  • Sharing those documents among and between care settings is also largely dependent upon legacy fax machines, which can cause frustration for staff and patients alike, while also posing potential security risks.

    Customer Value Proposition: Healthcare-Enabled MFP Solution

    The Konica Minolta Healthcare-Enabled MFP Plus, Connected by Kno2®, provides you with all of the tools you need to quickly and securely capture, store and send patient information across the care continuum.

    Customer Value Proposition: EHR-Integrated Scanning

    • Point of service scanning of patient records is accelerated by patient lookup available right from the bizhub via the Kno2 API and the healthcare-Enabled MFP connector.
    • Document types and patient matching data are all driven by the EHR, providing quick indexing and reduced labor expense related to scanning, as well as rapid chart completion ensuring up to date patient information.

    Your value message for Managed Print Services in Healthcare

    Konica Minolta‛s Managed Print Services for the Healthcare enterprise provides overall fleet management of your networked and local printers, scanners, label printers, and other similar peripherals. We give you one source for service and support. We relieve one burden from your IT team. We make paying for the monthly costs of your print fleet much simpler and more transparent.

    Assessment Process
    Assessment options: Describe our assessment process.

    Konica Minolta conducts a thorough assessment of the following:

    • The hospital‛s current print environment, both clinical and administrative
    • EHR requirements and needs
    • Networked device output, usage patterns, and print volumes

    Assessment data is used to design an optimized placement and document production environment to reduce costs, enhance workflows, and maximize uptime, which is critical when patient care is at stake.


    Here is a breakdown of the assessment steps:

    • ASSESSMENT LAUNCH – Fleet Monitoring combined with a physical site survey and site mapping software identifies business processes, workflow challenges and how information moves through your healthcare organization.
    • COST COLLECTION – Costs associated with print, processes and how you manage and move information in your healthcare organization and across your network of care providers are gathered.
    • INFORMATION SHARING – Assessment results, data collected, processes and all costs are reviewed.
    • SOLUTIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS – Our team of experts recommend potential areas for:
      • Savings
      • Business Process Streamlining
      • Improved Efficiency
      • Waste Reduction
      • Process Error Reduction

    Overcoming Objections

    Use this guidance to handle objections during phone calls and appointments.


    Objection 1: I have no time‛ I‛m too busy now.

    Response

    Thanks for sharing your concern. I know your time is valuable. And I can help eliminate time wasting processes such as manually ordering printer supplies and placing service calls. I assure you that your time will be well spent in discovering how to automate these processes for your hospital.

    I am only asking for 20 minutes. Which day and time will work for you?


    Objection 2: We have a staff in-house to fix printers.

    Response 1

    Thanks for sharing that with me. A number of our customers have IT staff who handle this. Who, may I ask, orders supplies and maintenance? What more could they accomplish for your organization if they did not spend time to manage printers and printer service?

    Which day and time will work for you to learn more?

    Response 2

    Thanks, I understand. Did you know that research studies have estimated that at least 10% of IT staff time could be returned to address more strategic duties if print management was not part of the IT staff workload. I am offering you a valuable opportunity to spend a relatively small amount of time that could lead to big gains for you.


    Objection 3: I don‛t think we spend very much on printers now.

    Response

    This is a common assumption. Many hospitals base this perspective on a misunderstanding of the full cost of printers. Can you share with me your cost of toner, maintenance kits and service so that I can calculate your actual cost and compare it to the cost of our program? This will validate your assumption or provide an alternative cost–saving option for you.

    Qualifying Questions
    Qualifying Questions
    1. How are you currently managing your printers and multifunction devices?
    2. Do you know what you spend on print each month? per year?
    3. How important is it to reduce the costs of your print environment?
    4. How much of your IT staff time is spent responding to printer issues?
    5. How much printer down time do you experience because you run out of supplies or experience breakdowns?
    6. How do you know if your printers and MFDs are overused or under–used?

    9. Have you quantified the impact?
    10. Are your print costs based on a monthly print volume?
    11. Are your print costs based on a count of color and black–and–white prints?
    12. Who services your print devices? How satisfied are you with the service you receive?
    13. Are your monthly invoices for print clear and easy to understand?
