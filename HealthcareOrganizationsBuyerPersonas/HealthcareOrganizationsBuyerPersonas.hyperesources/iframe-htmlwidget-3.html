Description

A Chief Technology Officer leads the work to identify short–term and long–term technology needs. A Chief Technology Officer oversees the Clinical, Biomedical Engineering, and Information Technology functions.

Responsibilities

The CTO is responsible for managing the technical services and the day–to–day delivery of all infrastructure technical services and operational support. Here are some examples:

  • Design the technology roadmap in support of clinical, financial and strategic goals.
  • Analyze, evaluate, and propose network, security, telecommunications, storage, database and technology architecture.
  • Evaluate additional software and hardware for usefulness and compatibility.
  • Monitor system utilization and capacity and provide capacity planning for acquiring and updating technology assets. Develop policies and procedures for the use of technology to enhance services for patients. A CTO may also have some management and financial responsibility for soliciting suppliers and acquiring technology assets.

Top Concerns

Evaluation of the network, communications, security, database, and storage technology that impacts patient care. Evaluation of software and hardware for usefulness and compatibility. Monitoring and reporting of technology utilization and capacity. Monitoring effectiveness of technology to enhance services that focus on patient care. Management of policies and procedures for the use of technology by the staff.
