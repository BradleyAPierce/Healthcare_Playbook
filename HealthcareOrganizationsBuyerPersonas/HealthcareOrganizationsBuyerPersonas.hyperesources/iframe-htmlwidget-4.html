Description

Chief Information Security Officer is a leader of practices to ensure HIPAA compliance and protection of patient health information.

Responsibilities

The CISO must anticipate new threats to the security of patient information and the EHR system. The CISO must lead efforts to prevent breaches of information security from happening. CISO duties can include any of the following:

  • Overseeing identity and access management regarding the EHR system and any technology that handles patient health information
  • Testing of systems to ensure that the protection of patient health information is robust
  • Reviewing investigations after breaches or incidents that reveal vulnerabilities
  • Implementation of an effective process for security incident reporting
  • Implementation and testing of disaster recovery and business continuity plans
  • Keeping current with the latest cybersecurity technology and best practices
  • Manage the information security team
  • Ensure compliance with government regulations
  • Planning the strategy behind, and design of, information security systems, policies, and best practices
  • Implementation of security awareness training for employees

Top Concerns

  • Maintaining the clinical staff‛s strict compliance with HIPAA
  • Ensuring that all personnel conform to the policies and practices that protect patient health information
  • Managing the timely reporting and investigation of any theft of PHI or breach of the security protocols
  • Fines resulting from HIPAA noncompliance
    • theft of PHI due to malicious acts
    • exposure of PHI due to lack of security controls or unauthorized access
    • preventing financial damage due to lawsuits caused by exposure or theft of PHI
    • damage to reputation of the system caused by theft or exposure of PHI
