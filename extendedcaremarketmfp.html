Konica Minolta's Healthcare-enabled MFP Addresses Challenges Providers Face Capturing and Exchanging Patient Information

Healthacre News

Ramsey, NJ, (April 5, 2022) Konica Minolta Business Solutions U.S.A., Inc. (Konica Minolta) is proud to announce the release of its latest version of the Healthcare-enabled MFP Plus, connected by Kno2. This cloud-based solution optimizes Konica Minolta bizhub multifunction printers (MFPs) for the healthcare industry. Specifically, it helps healthcare providers easily and securely exchange patient information electronically in formats that support and adhere to the latest industry regulations. These include the 21st Century Cares Act and HIPAA, as well as the most recent FCC regulations to reduce the use of standard copper phone lines.

Document Workflow and Connectivity Challenges

  • Scanning documents to a patient's health record can be a complicated and disjointed process, often requiring multiple steps and unnecessary time indexing to the EHR, resulting in additional operational costs.
  • Sharing those documents among and between care settings is also largely dependent upon legacy fax machines, which can cause frustration for staff and patients alike, while also posing potential security risks.

    • Customer Value Proposition: Healthcare-Enabled MFP Solution



    Rollover to scroll information

    Customer Value Proposition: Healthcare-Enabled MFP Solution

    The Konica Minolta Healthcare-Enabled MFP Plus, Connected by Kno2®, provides you with all of the tools you need to quickly and securely capture, store and send patient information across the care continuum.


    Customer Value Proposition: EHR-Integrated Scanning



    Rollover to scroll information

    Customer Value Proposition: EHR-Integrated Scanning

    • Point of service scanning of patient records is accelerated by patient lookup available right from the bizhub via the Kno2 API and the healthcare-Enabled MFP connector.
    • Document types and patient matching data are all driven by the EHR, providing quick indexing and reduced labor expense related to scanning, as well as rapid chart completion ensuring up to date patient information.

    Customer Value Proposition: Sharing Patient Information



    Rollover to scroll information

    Customer Value Proposition: Sharing Patient Information

    • Direct Secure Messaging provides a healthcare industry-standard to securely communicate sensitive PHI (protected health information) with other healthcare providers who use Direct exchange across the nation's largest clinical network.
    • For healthcare organizations that have not yet begun taking advantage of Direct exchange, HIPAA-compliant cloud fax is available for patient-centric faxing.

    • Ongoing labor shortages and growing cost of labor
    • Maintaining high utilization and maximizing patient referrals from other healthcare providers

    Handling Objections

    Objection: This sounds too complicated and unaffordable for us.

    Response: I can assure you that it is less complicated than you think. We have experience helping providers like you implement solutions for care transition, sharing patient information, and intake of patients or residents. With our expertise, you can enhance care coordination, reduce costs, maximize staff productivity, while ensuring HIPAA compliance.


    Objection: We're all set - we have a good vendor for our MFPs

    Response: That's great. May I ask - does your vendor help you save staff time and increase productivity in functions such as sharing patient information with your referral partners? Does your vendor help you seamlessly integrate documents from your referral partners into your E H R system?

    Qualifying Questions
    Qualifying Questions
    1. Do you regularly send / receive patient referrals?
    2. How much time do you spend processing inbound / outbound referrals?

    7. What is your process for onboarding new patients and residents?
    8. Which EHR are you using?
    9. Are you currently in the market for office technology?
    10. Do your current MFPs integrate seamlessly with your EHR?
