Here are several key challenges to managing IT in Acute Care.

  1. Consolidation of multiple facilities results in multiple separate networks that may be inefficient and that increase the IT workload.
  2. Multiple facilities can stretch the IT staff and make it difficult to find sufficient time to focus on strategic planning and projects.
  3. Mergers and acquisitions lead to multiple vendors and support contracts that are difficult to manage.
  4. Increasing complexity of systems and networks increases the cost of IT and requires more expertise on the IT staff.
  5. An understaffed Help Desk attempting to handle a high volume of support calls leads to service delays and dissatisfied clinical workers.
  6. IT Systems must support critical applications even during periods of upgrades and maintenance.

Decision Makers‛ Top–of–Mind Challenges
  1. System and server downtime must be minimized to keep essential applications and transactions running.
  2. The rapid pace of advances in emerging IT make it difficult to keep up with new technology that will be necessary in the future.
  3. There is limited time available for strategic planning and projects to improve systems.
  4. Acute Care mergers and acquisitions increase the complexity of managing and upgrading IT assets.
  5. It is challenging to sustain an expert IT staff that can troubleshoot problems and assure quality.
  6. Ongoing maintenance of clinical and administrative user PCs and tablets is extremely time consuming, yet essential.
  7. The Help Desk is swamped with calls for support, making it difficult to reduce the time needed to resolve and close support tickets.
  8. Extreme weather events and cyber attacks require plans and tactics for recovery and continuity in the event of damage to facilities and equipment failure.

Reduce Downtime Value Statement

We offer comprehensive server management that proactively updates and backs up servers remotely. In addition, remote monitoring detects issues before they cause serious breakdowns.

Emerging Technologies and Strategic Planning Value Statement

We provide strategic planning expertise. Our engineers have vast experience to help an Acute Care organization plan a strategy that accounts for the customer‛s IT budget as well as the emerging technology needed to achieve the organization‛s goals.

Proactive management of PCs and mobile devices Value Statement

We can perform proactive management of computing devices to support the Acute Care IT staff with operating system updates, security patch management, software license management, and even web content filtering.

Help Desk Value Statement

Our Help Desk service augments the capabilities of the in-house IT staff. Each Acute Care client directs their support needs to a dedicated core team. This arrangement means that our teams become familiar with each hospital‛s situation and needs. The Help Desk is available 24X7X365, and is easily accessible via phone hotline, email, or via a client portal. Our staff of technicians and engineers provide level 1, level 2, and level 3 technical support.

Troubleshooting and Quality Assurance Value Statement

Our remote support centers monitor the performance of networks and servers, and keep end–user computers running smoothly. They also provide IT support engineers to troubleshoot issues and provide assistance by phone or chat. When hospital staff need hands–on support, an All Covered field engineer can visit the site in person.

Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity Value Statement

Our backup service starts with a dedicated local appliance to compress and encrypt data for Windows and Linux platforms. Our service can also replicate data to a compliant data center maintained by All Covered.

Handling Objections on the Phone


Objection 1

Just send me some information.


Response: I would like to do that for you. I don‛t yet know enough about you to send information that would be most relevant to you. The meeting I asked for is for me to learn more about your goals – It‛s not a project pitch at all. I am confident that you will find value in a conversation with me. I suggest we meet on [day and time] next week. Does that work for you?




Objection 2

Response: I understand. Many of my current clients said the same thing when I first contacted them. Later they found that it‛s really helpful to have experts to contact in a pinch, because they could not predict when something would go wrong. The worst time to look for help is in the middle of an emergency. If you give me 15 minutes of your time, I am confident that it will be time well spent. I suggest we meet on [day and time] next week. Does that work for you?

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. How do you currently handle network support and resolution of problems?
  2. What are the most significant challenges with your current network infrastructure?
  3. What specific support requests does your Help Desk most frequently handle?
  4. Can you describe the objectives of your current IT upgrades or projects? How are your upgrades / projects progressing?
  5. Can you describe how you develop your information technology budget?
  6. How do you select and implement an Electronic Health Records system?
  7. How does your current Electronic Health Records system help you reach your goals and deliver value to your organization?
  8. Does your IT team have the expertise and depth of experience to manage your EHR system?
  9. How do you manage the inventory of all your network assets and end–user devices?
  10. Do you manage networks and devices in multiple locations? If YES, what are the challenges involved?
