Phone Scripts

Use phone scripts to stay focused on what is important to the prospect, to be consistent in what you say, to remain in control of the conversation, and to provide information to help a prospect decide to meet with you.

Phone Scripts
Phone Scripts
Buyer Personas

Buyer Persona: CIO, Chief Medical Information Officer, Chief Technology Officer, Director for Admission, Intake Manager


Phone Script

Hello [insert name of prospect].


My name is [insert your name], and I‛m a [insert your expertise] at Konica Minolta. Thank you for taking my call. I am sure you are busy, and I respect your time, so I‛ll be brief.


If you could implement a process to improve patient experience, what value would that bring to your organization? (Pause for reply)


We are talking to healthcare leaders about digital tools and automation that can reduce patient wait times, streamline the workflow of patient intake or admission, and provide metrics in useful reports that help managers monitor improvements.

I would like to set up a brief discussion with you about this. We may be able to help you solve challenges in this area.


I have time next Tuesday at 10:45 or Thursday at 1:15. Which time will work better for you to meet with me?

(Confirm Interest. If prospect asks for more information you may respond as follows:

We offer a patient experience management solution that tracks patient status, can drastically reduce wait times, and can update staff with alerts from a messaging platform. The solution also integrates with most Electronic Health Records systems.)


(Close for the appointment. Then thank the prospect and end the call.)

Qualifying Questions

Use this information when you: 1. Prepare for your appointments 2. Prepare your Value Statements 3. Choose Konica Minolta and All Covered content to share with prospects.

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. Do you currently have a workflow and system to alert managers and management on patient wait times?
  2. Can you monitor performance metrics across all sites, departments, and users to identify bottlenecks in real–time and take action BEFORE waiting times exceed key performance thresholds established by each facility?
  3. Do you have a system to generate patient tickets that can streamline their experience at your diagnostic centers, nurses‛ stations, surgery reception, and other locations of services?
  4. Do you have a system to generate way–finding information for patients?
  5. Do you have a system to alert and send patient status notifications via email or text message to the clinicians and receptionists in your facility? How would this capability improve your outcomes?
  6. Do you have a system to send patient status updates via email or text message to patient family members who are waiting?
  7. Do you have a system to prioritize multiple waiting lists, route patients to registrars based on required skill sets and patient preferences? How would this capability impact your personnel?
  8. Do you have a system that provides a display board in the waiting room to direct patients and provide real–time status updates to family members? How would this capability impact your community relations?
  9. Can you set up a workflow in your system to proactively contact patients to schedule appointments and follow–up consults in predefined intervals based on diagnosis or other criteria? How would this capability improve patient experience?
