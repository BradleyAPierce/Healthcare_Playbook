Summary of Print Management challenges

  1. Over reliance on printing across the healthcare enterprise is an obstacle to effective use of electronic health records (EHR) systems
  2. Risk of exposing printed patient health records to unauthorized personnel
  3. Wasted paper from print jobs left unclaimed in output trays
  4. Printing to less cost–effective output devices, such as desktop printers, instead of directing users to more cost effective devices
  5. Proper allocation of the costs of print to departments or functional teams
  6. Lower insurance reimbursement of claims leading to cost–cutting reviews of print expenses
  7. Multiple technology vendors and service plans that come with mergers and acquisitions of hospitals into larger enterprises

How You Can Add Value

Does your Acute Care customer require any of the following listed below?

Listen for clues to start a business conversation about how you can help them improve their current situation.


Managing costs of print

You can discuss how our Managed Print Services program can help your customer obtain visibility over all printing, and reduce or eliminate costly print devices.


Tracking print jobs and reporting on print usage

You can offer the ability to allocate print costs to specific budgets, and create reports on print usage from a dashboard. Your customer benefits from a centralized view of printing done by the entire organization.


Rules‛ based Printing

You can offer the capability to enforce duplex printing, restrict color prints to specific output devices, and route large print jobs to high‛volume MFPs.


Secure Print

You can offer device management solutions that include secure print release integrated with EHR systems. With this technology, your customer can drive accountability and enhanced security for all printing.

Qualifying Questions

Use this information when you: 1. Prepare for your appointments 2. Prepare your Value Statements 3. Choose Konica Minolta and All Covered content to share with prospects.

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. How do you monitor the associated costs of maintaining your output devices?
  2. How important is it to reduce the costs associated with printing?
  3. How do you capture staff use of MFPs so that the printing and copying costs can be allocated to departments or functional teams?
  4. How do you integrate the allocation of print costs into your management of your output devices?
  5. Do you have a goal for improving allocation of costs associated with printing and copying? What is the goal? Why is this goal important to you?
  6. What built–in safeguards have you deployed to ensure that only authorized personnel use your output devices?
  7. What features do you have to help you maintain document confidentiality when printing at any MFP on your network?
  8. How much are you using preprinted forms?
  9. Have you developed an initial budget for your forms management program? How did you develop that budget?
  10. Do you need to limit employee print and copy to specific MFPs or printers?
  11. Can anyone walk up to an MFP and make a copy?
  12. How do you currently limit color printing to only essential uses?
  13. Do you need to enforce two–sided printing and copying?
  14. How much of your IT Help Desk time and effort is devoted to printer issues?
