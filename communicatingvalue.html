How to communicate value
when Prospecting

A Value Statement is a business statement that tells what positive impact you or your company can make to improve a customer's business.

3 Parts to Communicating Value
to your Healthcare accounts

Communicating Value: Part 1

Three Components of A Value Statement

Here is a simple, three–part formula for an effective value statement.

Communicating Value: Part 1

Here is a simple, three-part formula for an effective value statement:





Captivate
  • Captivate is the hook. It is the idea that conveys what you can do for the prospect.
  • When you captivate, the prospect should respond -
      • I am interested

Differentiate
  • Differentiate is why or how your offering is better than other offerings.
  • When you differentiate, the prospect should respond -
      • I see the difference in your offering

Validate
  • Validate is the proof that you can create a positive outcome for the prospect.
  • When you validate, the prospect should respond -
      • I believe you can deliver the solution to my problem


You need all three components, because:

  • If you do not captivate the prospect, you won’t stimulate any interest.
  • If you captivate, but do not differentiate, the prospect will doubt that you are any better than your competitors.
  • If you captivate and differentiate, but do not validate, the prospect may not trust you enough to do business with you.
Communicating Value: Part 2

Best Practice for Writing Value Statements

Here is a process you can use and repeat to write value statements for prospecting and appointments.

Communicating Value: Part 2
Best Practice for Writing Value Statements



Here is a process you can use and repeat to write value statements for prospecting and appointments.


Step 1: Use an action word early in statement

Example of an action word - Protect


Step 2: Use a primary keyword or key phrase that will be acted upon

Example of primary keyword or key phrase - Patient information


Step 3: State a positive outcome

Two examples of positive outcomes - Prevent unauthorized access and Maintain compliance with HIPAA


Here is a sample Value Statement based on the 3 Steps:

We help hospitals protect1 their patient information2 with security measures that will prevent unauthorized access3 to health records and maintain HIPAA compliance4 .


In the above statement:

  1. Protect is the action word
  2. Patient information is the primary key phrase
  3. Prevent unauthorized access is one positive outcome
  4. Maintain HIPAA compliance is a second positive outcome

More Value Statements

We help hospitals secure their documents by providing scanning and workflow solutions that improve productivity and control costs.

We help hospitals and health systems manage information effectively and securely with our content management solutions and cloud services, so that they can focus on the patient care.

Communicating Value: Part 3

Decision-making Drivers and Buyer Personas

A decision maker has a Buyer Persona that is defined by Financial Targets & Operational Outcomes

Communicating Value: Part 3
Decision–Making Drivers and Buyer Personas



A decision maker has a Buyer Persona that is defined by:

  1. Role in the organization, and
  2. Decision drivers – the factors that drive decision making

For simplicity, we describe two categories of decision drivers:

  • Financial Targets
  • Operational Outcomes

Here are two examples of decision drivers for an Acute Care Buyer Persona:

Example 1:

Director of Administration / Director of Finance

  • Financial Targets
    • Prioritize departmental spending
    • Align departmental budgets to achieve law firm goals
  • Operational Outcomes
    • Execute business priorities identified by senior leadership
    • Create and enforce operational policies
    • Oversee day to day business operations
    • Ensure departmental alignment with goals and objectives of the hospital


Example 2:

Chief Information Officer

  • Financial Targets
    • Maximizing investment dollars placed in IT
    • Ensure that IT spending aligns with business goals
  • Operational Outcomes
    • Mitigate risks to IT infrastructure
    • Develop and maintain compliance and security
    • Ensure reliability of IT assets
    • Cultivate high level of IT staff talent
