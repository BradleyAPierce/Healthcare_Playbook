What is Intelligent Information Management and Document Management?

  • Part 1
  • Part 2
IIM and Document Management refer to digital methods and tools for controlling the following:
  • Managing the document lifecycle
  • Access to documents
  • Security to protect documents
  • Sharing and routing documents
  • Archiving and retrieval of documents

IIM and Document Management can streamline tracking, approval, signature, and other document functions. This capability is particularly important in business office functions, such as Accounts Receivable, Accounts Payable, Revenue Cycle Management, Contracts, Compliance, and Human Resources.

The Electronic Health Records system that the healthcare organization uses may dictate what document management or IIM solution is the best fit. If, for example, Epic is the EHR, we may recommend Hyland OnBase as the IIM solution.

The goal of IIM and Document Management is to use digital information efficiently and help workers be more productive.

Summary of IIM & Document Management challenges

  1. Many content silos or separate systems of content
  2. Inadequate document management capabilities in project management systems and in records systems
  3. Slow document search and retrieval
  4. Document approval bottlenecks in workflow and business processes
  5. Manual paper process hinders efficiency and productivity
  6. Costly paper forms
  7. Space devoted to on–site storage of paper documents
  8. Storage costs for paper documents
  9. Too many single–function printers add to glut of paper

Qualifying Questions

Use this information when you: 1. Prepare for your appointments 2. Prepare your Value Statements 3. Choose Konica Minolta and All Covered content to share with prospects.

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. What are your biggest document challenges?
  2. How long does it take you to find a critical document?
  3. Have you had any problems with finding documents or with the speed of searching for documents, especially in clinical settings?
  4. How do you manage situations in which a critical document cannot be located?
  5. Describe the different document types that you process in your current workflow / workflows. How long does it take you to process [insert type of document here: claims, invoices, referrals, patient histories, etc]
  6. Are documents processed one–by–one (point of care)? Are documents processed in a batch?
  7. Are documents scanned and indexed at a central location, such as Health Information Management (HIM)?
  8. What kinds of physical actions do you currently perform on paper documents? For example, signatures, stamping, or other?
  9. What document process would you most like to automate?
  10. What are the main applications (EMR/EHR, HIS, PM, RCM, Lab, PACs, etc) that you use in your organization / your functional area / your department?
  11. Are there any organizational goals tied to the rollout of a document management or content management system?
  12. What is your records retention policy?
  13. Are you storing documents on–site, off–site, or both? If you use off–site document storage, how much do you pay to store paper records?
  14. Who makes sure your document content is secure?
