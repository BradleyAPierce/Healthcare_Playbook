How to Research a
Healthcare Account

A Value Statement is a business statement that tells what positive impact you or your company can make to improve a customer's business.

6 Steps to use while
Researching Healthcare accounts

CRM

Always build a knowledge base from CRM. Follow the steps to do this.

CRM
How to Research a Healthcare Account


Step 1
  • Navigate to the D&B company record, integrated with your CRM.
  • Use the information in the following tabs:
    • Description
    • Family Tree
    • People
    • Call Prep
    • News
Step 2: On the Description tab:
  • This tab gives a street address, the account‛s DUNS number, a phone number, social media presence, and a web URL.
  • Make a note of the healthcare account website so you can continue your research there.
  • Look at the Location Type to see if the healthcare organization is at a single–location, or at multiple locations.
  • Look at the number of employees at all sites.



Step 3: On the Family Tree tab:

If the healthcare account has multiple locations, view the locations here. Some may be recent acquisitions that are near you.





Step 4: On the People tab:

Note the titles of the people listed. When you find managers, leaders and top executives listed, you should view their LinkedIn profiles.





Step 5: On the Call Prep tab:

Choose an industry description in this tab to view talking points and challenges. Review the facts and the Executive Talking Points for information you can use in a prospecting phone call or email. Review the Key Business Challenges and Key Business Opportunities for ideas that will help you form questions to ask during a prospecting phone call to ask for an appointment.





Step 6: On the News tab:

Look for information about technology, new facilities, acquisitions, or new leadership.

Definitive Healthcare

Definitive Healthcare is a technology company that provides intelligence on the healthcare market.

Definitive Healthcare
Directions: Follow the steps to find healthcare data provided by Definitive.


Step 1:

Select an account in CRM360 and navigate to the D&B tab.



Definitive Step 2:

Select the D&B tab and the Definitive Healthcare frame will open to its Summary tab. This tab provides general information such as organization name and type of organization, such as hospital, ambulatory practice, or extended care facility. The Summary tab also displays key clinical metrics, such as number of affiliated physicians.





*Please note that there may be accounts with no Definitive Healthcare information within the D&B tab. In such instances, please note the account name and CRM ID and send email to jcisna@kmbs.konicaminolta.us and/or nbalakrishnaraja@kmbs.konicaminolta.us. We will work with Definitive to make the match.


Note the other tabs in the Definitive Healthcare frame. These include Executives, Financial and Clinical Metrics, Affiliations, News, Technologies, and Purchasing/Staffing.



Step 3:

On the Executives tab and scroll through the list. Definitive also provides an extensive list of key contacts, including executives. In many cases there are phone numbers and email address. These contacts go deeper than the typical CEO, COO, CIO, and provide Chief Medical Information Officer (CMIO), Chief Nursing Officer (CNO), Innovation leaders, Directors of Health Information Management or HIM, and many other relevant key contacts.





Step 4:

Select the Technologies tab. Use it to determine the various technologies in place at the customer. This tab first provides the top technologies at a glance, such as the EHR, and the years implemented.






Step 5:

Scroll down the Technologies tab to Current Implementations. This is a list of IT systems by category, technology, vendor, and product. You may filter for the specific systems you are interested in finding, such as inpatient and outpatient EHR, document management, health information exchange, and so on.



LinkedIn

Add depth to your knowledge from CRM by using LinkedIn profiles.

LinkedIn
Step 1

Visit the account‛s LinkedIn page. Look for links to the leadership or professional staff.


Step 2

Visit the LinkedIn profiles of the leaders that you found in D&B People.


Step 3

Review each area of the profile to discover each leader‛s expertise and prior healthcare experience.


Step 4

Look at the individual‛s connections to see if any work for other accounts that are in your CRM account database. If the individual has a connection in your LinkedIn network, you should ask your network connection to make an introduction.


Step 5

Look at the prospect‛s membership in LinkedIn Groups – this will indicate areas of professional interest.

Customer Websites & Social Media

Visit the healthcare account website for timely information and news.

Customer Websites & Social Media
Research Customer Websites & Social Media


Part 3: Use the healthcare account website to continue your research.


Step 1

Review the Home or About page for a summary. The About page may have information on facilities, locations, and leadership.

Step 2

The website may also have links to Blogs, Videos, Podcasts, and Events. Any of these can offer information you can use in prospecting messages.

Step 3

Look for a News section about the hospital, facility, or practice. The news section may have updates about new facilities, acquisitions, and new leadership.

Step 4

Look for a directory or a link to senior leadership. There may be leaders listed that you did not find in LinkedIn.

Step 5

Look for an Annual Report, which may contain updates about technology and facilities you can use in your prospecting messages and appointments.



Part 4: Social media

Use other social media, such as Twitter, YouTube, and Facebook, to discover news about the healthcare account.

Many healthcare organizations today use multiple social media platforms to communicate.

Becker‛s Hospital Review

Becker‛s Healthcare is the go–to source for healthcare decision–makers and one of the fastest growing media platforms in the industry.

Becker‛s Hospital Review

Becker‛s Hospital Review is a media platform that provides information for health care leaders and decision makers. You can search for news about specific hospitals on its website. The Becker‛s Hospital Review website is one place to find hospital news that may not be covered in a hospital‛s own website.
