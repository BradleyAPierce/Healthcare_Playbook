Summary of cybersecurity challenges for Extended Care providers

  • Increasing cyberattacks that target health records
  • Increasing sophistication of cyberattacks
  • Growing numbers of devices connected to wired and wireless healthcare networks
  • Employee awareness of the risks of cyberattacks
  • Comprehensive employee training to prevent data breaches
  • Increasing complexity of systems that must be protected
  • Increase in mobile technology that gives employees access to health records
  • Federal and state rules governing protection of health records
  • Proactive security and compliance audits by government
  • Ability of internal IT teams to manage security requirements
  • Ability of hospital personnel to identify the source of a security breach

Cyberattacks can cripple health systems


After a May 31, 2021 cyberattack, University of Florida Health Central Florida facilities faced an interruption of their IT systems, and suspended all computer connections to other UF Health campuses in Gainesville and Jacksonville Florida. The health system also switched clinicians to paper documentation methods. The cyberattack compromised patient information including names, addresses, birth dates, Social Security numbers, and health insurance details. The health system‛s Electronic Health Records and other IT systems were down for almost one month.


San Diego–based Scripps Health was hit with a ransomware attack on May 1, 2021. The attack compelled Scripps to take a portion of its IT system offline, disrupting patient care and forcing medical personnel to use paper records. The attack compromised the data on almost 150,000 patients. Stolen data included names and Social Security numbers. Now Scripps has been hit with lawsuits claiming that the system is liable for damages.

Handling Objections

Objection 1: “Send me some information.”


Response

Thank you–I'm glad you would like to know more. The meeting we're asking for would not be a product pitch. We don't know enough about you yet to presume to know what is relevant. I only request a brief meeting to share best practices around security assessments and services. What does your schedule look like on Monday or Tuesday next week?


Objection 2: “We handle all our cybersecurity in house.”


Response

That's great! Many of our current customers do as well. What they have found is that with the many systems and devices that need protection, it's helpful to have additional experts to ensure you can meet all security requirements.

Often, we can enhance the practices you are already following.

Who on your team would be interested in talking about this in greater detail?


Objection 3: “We already have a company handling our cybersecurity strategy.”


Response

I understand, I'm glad to hear you've leveraged help in order to manage this critical challenge in today's world. Many of our existing customers also had previously hired external help and what they found was that after some baseline assessments, they discovered that there were areas of exposure that they weren't initially aware of.


At the very least, speaking with one of our Cybersecurity experts can give you the peace of mind that you truly are protected. It never hurts to have a backup plan if your primary plan fails.

Who on your team would be interested in talking about this in greater detail?

Qualifying Questions

Use this information when you: 1. Prepare for your appointments 2. Prepare your Value Statements 3. Choose Konica Minolta and All Covered content to share with prospects.

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. How often do you perform scheduled technical vulnerability assessments for your network?
  2. When was your most recent security assessment?
  3. Based on those assessments, what have you learned about vulnerabilities in your network?
  4. Do you know all the assets connected to your network, wired and wireless, including medical devices and what their vulnerabilities are?
  5. How do you currently monitor the current state of your security protections for patient records?
  6. How frequently do you apply security patches and updates in your systems?
  7. Do you have a compliance portal with a dashboard that shows a view of the current state of both privacy and security risk assessments?
  8. Can you describe your current security incident response plan?
  9. Can you describe your current threat management plan?
  10. If a data breach does occur, can you demonstrate that appropriate security controls, policies, and procedures were in place?
  11. If a data breach occurs, how do you identify the source and correct the problem?
  12. If a data breach occurs, do you have a reliable and tested mechanism to contact patients and execute a breach notification?
  13. Do you have adequate data backup and a plan for business recovery in the event of an attack that causes your system to be offline?
  14. Do you have documented ongoing security awareness training for hospital employees?
  15. Do you monitor your security policies and procedures updates with audit log changes?
  16. How often do you review network access and access privileges to critical applications?
  17. If you engage with third party suppliers, do you have documentation that they are complying with HIPAA per your Business Associate Agreement (BAA)?
