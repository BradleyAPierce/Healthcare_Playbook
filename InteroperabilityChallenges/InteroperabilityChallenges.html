Incomplete PHILack of integration between systems used by different providers means the patient record in one facility may be incomplete. This hinders effective patient care.
Security RisksLack of integration between providers exposes patient records to a manual exchange workflow with the risk of security breaches.
DelaysLack of integration between providers may mean delays in exchange of patient records, which adversely affect care and increase patient dissatisfaction with service.
Secure Exchange of PHI with Non-Acute FacilitiesMany non-Acute and post-Acute providers lack integration with the EHR systems of affiliated hospitals. This is a barrier to digital, secure exchange of PHI when a hospital patient is discharged to a non-Acute provider. This lack is also a barrier when a non-Acute provider must send a patient or resident to the hospital.

Unsecured FaxTo share patient records with network partners, some providers rely mainly on older fax communication. A legacy fax connection only provides a phone number, but not necessarily the identification of the sender or receiver.
Higher OverheadAny manual steps that must be performed during the exchange of patient information can lead to higher overhead costs.
