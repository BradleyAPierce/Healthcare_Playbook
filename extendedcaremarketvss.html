Visible Weapon Detection Use Case

Here are several key challenges to managing Physical Security.

  • Managing the flow of visitors, staff, patients, and others at facility entry points with diminishing labor resources at higher costs.
  • Securing highly sensitive areas within the facility that contain PHI or other critical information or substances, such as medications (particularly controlled substances).
  • Protecting patients and staff is top of mind.
  • Security needs to be ensured with enhanced secure entry without impeding the level of access.
  • There needs to be a scalable way of screening & reporting patient, staff, and visitor vaccination status for government health code compliance.
  • Remove the need for dedicated employees or 3rd party contractors who are hired to perform security screening.
  • Slow and manual visitor management.

Handling Objections

Objection 1: "Our current system is sufficient for our needs."
Response: "I understand your current system might meet basic needs. However, our solution offers advanced features like real-time monitoring and analytics, which enhance security and operational efficiency. Could I show you how these features might add value in ways your current system might not?"

Objection 2: "We're concerned about the cost of upgrading."
Response: "That's a valid concern. It's worth considering the long-term savings in security management and efficiency improvements. Also, we can explore government grants and funding options available for healthcare facilities to offset the initial investment."

Objection 3: "We're worried about the complexity of new technology."
Response: "Our solutions are designed with user-friendliness in mind. We provide comprehensive training and support to ensure a smooth transition. Can I arrange a demo to showcase how easy integrating and using our system is?"

Objection 4: "We must maintain strict compliance with healthcare regulations."
Response: "Our system is built to comply with healthcare regulations, including patient privacy laws. We can provide detailed information on how our solution meets these regulations and enhances compliance reporting."

Objection 5: "We don't have the staff to manage a new system."
Response: "Our solution is designed to reduce the workload on your staff. Features like automated reporting and alerts can free up your staff for more critical tasks. Would it be helpful to see a case study where we helped a similar facility streamline its security management?"

Qualifying Questions

Use this information when you: 1. Prepare for your appointments 2. Prepare your Value Statements 3. Choose Konica Minolta and All Covered content to share with prospects.

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. Do they currently have a secure entry system?
  2. Any issues with that system?
  3. What safety and security protocols / processes do they have?
  4. What protocols/processes are in place for incidents? (active shooter, fire, tornado, etc.)
  5. Are there any security lapses that they want to eliminate / reduce? (lost badges, keys)
  6. Do they have an elopement issue?
  7. Do they want contactless entry?
  8. How many employees & residents need to be screened/verified?
  9. How many buildings are to be monitored?
  10. Do they have to comply with government health mandates? i.e. need automated reporting and logging
  11. Do they want second factor authentication with their existing secure entry system?
  12. Have they looked into government grants for security equipment upgrades?
  13. Do they have a screening bottleneck issue or visitor check-in issue?
