Overview of Layered Security

Device security provides users with multiple methods to ensure the safety of solid-state drives, mitigate the risk of network viruses, and detect any unauthorized tampering or hacking of the firmware.

With the layered security approach for MFPs, customers are offered the flexibility to tailor their device security according to their specific needs. This approach empowers customers to customize the level of security measures in place, whether it be to defend against network breaches, system hacks, unauthorized access, or inadvertent data exposure (e.g., leaving sensitive documents unattended in the output tray).

The first security layer offered is the bizhub SECURE portfolio. Numerous clients are unaware that configuring the bizhub MFP is essential to mitigate vulnerabilities. Without proper configuration, there is a risk of unauthorized access to data at rest or potential infiltration of the internal network through the MFP.

The second layer that adds onto the bizhub SECURE portfolio is the Konica Minolta developed and embedded device-based security monitoring MarketPlace based APP called bizhub SECURE Notifier. This app makes it easy for our clients to check the status of their bizhub SECURE settings, including: · Administrator password · HDD/SSD encryption · HDD/SSD password protection · Auto document deletion · Temporary data overwrite (not applicable to i-Series SSD).

The bizhub SECURE Notifier app visually indicates whether or not the MFP is secure, lists the status of the security settings and allows print out status reports. Users can also be notified via email that their security settings have been compromised.

Building upon the foundation of bizhub SECURE and bizhub SECURE Notifier, we have further enhanced protection with the introduction of an added layer of protection with real-time Antivirus and Malware protection. By partnering with Bitdefender©, a worldwide leader in cybersecurity, a fully functional and feature-rich, on-board Antivirus and Malware solution for i-Series MFPs.

Main Benefits For the Customers

These describe 1.) How your services or solution can solve a challenge that your customer faces, or 2.) How your offering makes a positive impact on the customer`s organization or business process.

Main Benefits For the Customers

  • Confidentiality: Healthcare providers can have confidence that their sensitive documents and data are secure, reducing the risk of unauthorized access or data breaches.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Implementing robust data security measures helps customers meet compliance requirements mandated by regulations such as HIPAA, Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) and PCI-DSS, GDPR avoiding potential fines or penalties for non-compliance.
  • Protection of Patient Health Information: Healthcare providers can safeguard their PHI from theft or unauthorized disclosure, preserving their reputations, and business integrity.
  • Increased Trust and Reputation: Demonstrating a commitment to data security instills trust and confidence among patients, employees, and the larger community, enhancing the Healthcare provider's reputation and credibility in the marketplace.
  • Cost Savings: Proactively addressing data security concerns can help Healthcare providers avoid the financial costs associated with data breaches, such as legal fees, and regulatory fines.
  • Improved Productivity: Secure printing practices and streamlined document workflows enable customers to enhance productivity by minimizing the risk of document loss, ensuring timely access to critical information, and reducing manual intervention in document handling processes.
  • Simplified Compliance Audits: By implementing robust data security measures on MFPs, Healthcare providers can streamline compliance audits and reporting processes, saving time and resources required for demonstrating adherence to regulatory requirements.


Document Security:

  • Image Log Transfer File (ILTF) capability: The ILTF contains a record of all MFP user activity and PDF images of those activities for audit purposes so you know who and when to access information.
  • Security alerts: Customers can set up alerts via email to notify responsible recipients if a security breach has occurred.
  • Searchable archive: The customer has a full-text searchable PDF archive record of all MFP activities. An administrator can see what was printed, copied, scanned, faxed or emailed.

Qualifying Questions

When you know how to ask the right questions, you possess a proven method for uncovering needs, and creating a sense of urgency in the prospect to seek your help solving a problem. Use this information to plan effective appointments with your prospects.

Qualifying Questions
General Qualifying Questions
  • What are your organization's primary concerns or challenges regarding data security on MFPs?
  • Have you experienced any security incidents or data breaches related to MFP usage in the past?
  • Are you aware of compliance regulations or industry standards that apply to the security of data processed on MFPs in your organization?
  • What measures do you currently have in place to secure data on MFPs, such as user authentication, encryption, or access controls?
  • Are you interested in enhancing the security features of your existing MFPs or considering upgrading to newer models with advanced security capabilities?
  • Do you require integration with existing network security infrastructure, such as firewalls or intrusion detection systems, to protect MFPs from external threats?
  • What specific security features or functionalities are you looking for in MFPs to address your organization's data security needs?
  • How important is it for your organization to have real-time monitoring and alerts for potential security breaches or unauthorized access attempts on MFPs?

Common Business Problems and Challenges
Common Business Problems and Challenges


Of all the resources in legal, protecting a client's valuable information is paramount. Law firms are morally obligated to protect sensitive data, and have a strict legal and ethical responsibility to ensure compliance. The American Bar Association and other international entities have established guidelines and rules that mandate the secure handling of client information.

  1. Unauthorized Access: Concerns about unauthorized users gaining access to sensitive documents or confidential information stored on the MFP.
  2. Data Leakage: Risks of unintentional data leakage or exposure due to insecure printing practices, such as leaving printouts unattended on the output tray or printing sensitive information to shared printers.
  3. Firmware Security: Concerns about the security of the MFP's firmware and the potential for firmware tampering or hacking, which could compromise the device's functionality or enable unauthorized access.
  4. Compliance Requirements: Challenges in ensuring compliance with data protection regulations and industry standards (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) ) regarding the secure handling and storage of sensitive data processed by the MFP.
  5. Mobile Device Integration: Challenges related to securely integrating mobile devices (e.g., smartphones, tablets) with the MFP for printing or scanning purposes while maintaining data security and user authentication protocols.
  6. Secure Printing: Ensuring secure printing practices, such as implementing user authentication, encryption, and secure print release mechanisms, to prevent unauthorized individuals from accessing printed documents.
  7. Data Encryption: Concerns about the encryption of data stored on the MFP's internal storage or transmitted over the network to protect against data breaches or interception by malicious actors.
  8. End-of-Life Management: Challenges in securely decommissioning or disposing of MFPs at the end of their lifecycle to prevent data exposure or unauthorized access to residual data stored on the device's storage media.
  9. Personnel mishandling information, leaving it out on MFP.
  10. Network Vulnerabilities: Vulnerabilities in the MFP's network connectivity that could be exploited by cyber attackers to gain access to the organization's network or intercept data transmissions.
