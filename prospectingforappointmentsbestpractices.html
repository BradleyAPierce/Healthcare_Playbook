Email Scripts

This section presents an overview of best practices for email prospecting.

Email Scripts
Email Prospecting Best Practices


Your prospects expect you to bring them information that is of value to them. Email should be part of your prospecting arsenal because you can use it to share educational content, such as video, blogs, white papers, case studies, and brochures. When you share educational content, you start to build trust and you position yourself as an expert.



Best Practice: Clear and specific subject line

Prospects usually decide whether to open email based on the subject line. Make your subject lines brief and customer focused. Avoid a subject line that is about Konica Minolta.



Best Practice: Use a Value Statement

Your value statement in email should not be more than 25 words.


Example 1:

You can strengthen the security position of your hospital by applying protective measures that we recommend based on results of our threat assessment.




Example 2:

You can reduce the risk that your patient information is vulnerable to unauthorized access by deploying our robust security measures in your hospital network.




Best Practice: Share educational content

Educational content can be any of the following, and more:

  • A white paper about new technology
  • A blog or article about best practices in the legal industry
  • A case study or a customer success story
  • A video with a subject matter expert sharing knowledge or advice
  • Review the Key Resources page in this PlayBook to find content that you can use in your prospecting emails.

Best Practice: Call to Action

Offer to discuss options during an appointment with the prospect. Suggest specific times for a meeting. Ask which time best fits the schedule of the prospect.


Best Practice: Follow up

Always follow up a prospecting email about 2 days after you send it. Ask the prospect about the value of the educational content that you share. Ask to schedule an appointment.

You may follow up with a phone call or a voicemail. If your follow up is a voicemail, you will need to call back again in a few days.

You will find other sample email messages in this playbook. Check the pages in the Acute, Extended, and Ambulatory sections for email scripts tailored to the specific challenges.

Phone Scripts

This section presents an overview of best practices for phone prospecting.

Phone Scripts
Phone Prospecting Best Practices


Best Practice: Review Buyer Personas

Before you call, review the Buyer Persona of your prospects. Review LinkedIn profiles, as well as any other information from the web or social media. Don‛t forget to review the People tab in the Dun & Bradstreet section of your CRM.


Best Practice: Create a phone script for each Buyer Persona 7 Advantages of phone scripts:
  1. You stay focused on what is important to your prospect
  2. You provide information to help the prospect decide you offer something of value
  3. You are consistent in your message
  4. You have confidence to make an effective call
  5. You remain in control of the call
  6. You establish credibility with the prospect
  7. You don‛t waste the prospect‛s time – or your own

Value of phone scripts:

You can use scripts for one Buyer Persona in multiple phone calls with just minor adjustments. Why? Because a Buyer Persona in a hospital, such as a Chief Information Officer, shares many of the same challenges as other individuals with the same Buyer Persona.


Best Practice: Create 3 essential parts of your phone script:

1. Disarm the prospect

Suppose that your prospect answers your phone call. One thing is certain: the prospect is busy. The prospect may be reluctant to speak to you.

Introduce yourself and your company. Acknowledge that the prospect is busy with a statement of empathy.


Example:

“Hello [name of prospect]. This is [your name] from [Konica Minolta] [All Covered]. Thank you for taking my call. I am sure you are busy, and I respect your time, so I will be brief.”



2. State your Purpose

First, state the purpose of your call. You wish to set up an appointment.

Then, give the prospect a reason to make the appointment. This is when you use a Value Statement.



Example:

I have some valuable information to share. I am calling to schedule 20 minutes to meet with you next week to discuss how I can help you to reduce the costs of print 15–20%. My company has deep expertise in print management, and other hospitals have achieved great success with our help.



3. Ask for the appointment.

Give specific options for the appointment. Use language that will encourage the prospect to answer your question.


Example:

I am available next Tuesday at 9:45 or on Thursday at 1:15. Which time will work best for you?


Voicemail Scripts

This section presents an overview of best practices for voicemail messages.

Voicemail Scripts
Voicemail Prospecting Best Practices


Suppose your call goes to voicemail.

Best practice: You should create two versions of each phone script:

Version 1: Use when the prospect answers your call

Version 2: Use when you must leave a voicemail


Best Practice: Tips for Effective Voicemail
  • Use the name of the prospect when you begin the message.
  • Use a value statement early in the message.
  • State that your purpose is to schedule a meeting.
  • Repeat your phone number slowly.
  • Practice your voicemail script so that you can say it smoothly, without stumbles.
  • Do not ramble. Stick to your script.
  • Avoid saying things like “It‛s me again. I am sorry we haven‛t connected . . . ”

Here is a sample voicemail message based on the sample phone script:


Hello John,

This is Jordan Lincoln from [Konica Minolta] [All Covered].


I am calling to schedule 20 minutes to meet with you next week to discuss how I can help you to reduce costs in your print environment by 15–20%.


I think you will find value in exploring how your hospital might benefit. Please call me at 123–456–7890.


Again, my name is Jordan Lincoln, and you may reach me at 123–456–7890. Thank you.



You will find other sample scripts in this playbook. Check the pages in the Acute, Extended, and Ambulatory sections for phone scripts and voicemail scripts tailored to the specific challenges.


Best Practice: Work on your tone of voice

Be mindful of your tone of voice. Your tone can communicate your level of empathy, confidence, knowledge, and positive attitude. Prospects make judgments about you based on your tone of voice. Therefore, you want to sound pleasant, positive, and clear.


Tips for practicing tone of voice:

  • Stand up to speak.
  • Smile when you speak. Use a mirror to make sure you smile.
  • Record your message. Play it back to yourself. Ask a colleague or manager to listen to your recorded message, and give feedback.
