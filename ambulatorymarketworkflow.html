Overcoming Objections

Objection 1: "I have no time–I‛m too busy now."


Response

Thanks for sharing your concern. I know your time is valuable. In only 20 minutes, I can share information about how we streamline workflows for our customers, helping them gain efficiencies and reduce costs. I believe you will find this to be time well spent. Which day and time will work for you?


Objection 2: "I have a vendor – I‛m all set..."


Response 1

Thanks for sharing that with me. How is your vendor helping you assess your current workflow and productivity? I can offer you valuable information about our deep expertise in workflow automation. Which day and time will work for you to learn more?


Response 2

Thanks, I understand. Quick question– When was the last time a vendor provided an accurate assessment of your workflow and productivity so that you could make data–driven improvements in your business? I am offering you a valuable opportunity to spend a relatively small amount of time that could lead to big gains for you.


Response 3

Thanks, I understand. Quick question– Are you completely satisfied with your current vendor‛s performance? I assure you that your decision to meet with me briefly will pay off for you. Which day and time next week works best for you?

Qualifying Questions

Use this information when you: 1. Prepare for your appointments 2. Prepare your Value Statements 3. Choose Konica Minolta and All Covered content to share with prospects.

Qualifying Questions
Qualifying Questions
  1. Are there any specific challenges that you are trying to solve?
  2. Who typically feels the impact of these challenges?
  3. Have you quantified the impact?

    4. Process and Route

  4. How do you capture, process and route patient information?
  5. Can you estimate how many documents you process on a daily or monthly basis?
  6. Would it be helpful to be able to select where your documents go when you capture the document? Would it be helpful to determine precisely how the captured information is named and indexed?
  7. How do you notify the recipient of a document that is routed to a specific person or function?

    8. Scanning

  8. Are you scanning into an EHR/EMR or other key business or clinical application today? If so, can you explain the process?
  9. What kind of documents are you scanning?
  10. Do you scan a lot of insurance cards, ID cards or odd size documents?
  11. Is there a software or application that these scans reside in?
  12. Are you able to use MFPs to accomplish scanning?
  13. Are you capable of indexing to a patient‛s record from the scanner or MFP? If not, would that be valuable in your workflow?
  14. Do you scan a lot of double–sided documents and are they mixed with single–sided documents?

    15. Search and retrieval

  15. How do you currently search for and retrieve scanned documents?
  16. How do you share documents?
  17. What else would you like to do with your documents that you are unable to do now?

    18. Identify Needs

  18. Who is the best person to respond to technical questions about this? Who is the best person to discuss business requirements regarding the project?
  19. Have you developed an initial budget for this project?
  20. How did you get to that estimate?
  21. Do you have an initial timeline for this project?
  22. Do you have an executive sponsor?
  23. What is the process for your evaluation?
  24. Who‛s going to be involved?
  25. Who will make the final decision? What are the criteria to make a decision?
