Use of FaxReliance on analog fax transmission produces a large paper stream of patient and clinical information, such as inbound faxes from referral sources, and outbound faxes to other providers.
Mergers and Consolidation of FacilitiesMergers and consolidation of health networks results in complex or inefficient workflows with multiple processes, vendors, and support contracts.
ScanningManual and time–consuming scanning to get paper–based information into Electronic Health Records systems requires too much employee time. Document scanning that is restricted to a central scanning facility can cause delays in updating patient information in an Electronic Health Records system.
Reliance on Paper-Based InformationReliance on paper–based information during admission, transfer, and discharge of patients contributes to hybrid workflows that combine paper and Electronic Health Records.
Manual ProcessManual processing and indexing of documents leads to improper document naming, making it difficult to access critical patient information when needed.
